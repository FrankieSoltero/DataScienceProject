Project Goals


The goal of this project is to investigate whether climate change is as bad as it seems. My data set consists of all the average land temperatures around the globe, by every major city, by every country, by every city, and by every state. The goal will be to identify if there is tremendous increase in temperature from 1750 to today and to identify what caused those temperature increases. The data is sourced from https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/berkeleyearth/climate-change-earth-surface-temperature-data?resource=download. My hypothesis is that every country, city, major city, state, and the world as a whole will have a fairly large temperature increase from the first recorded temperatures. With a goal in mind to try and predict the global increase and country increase for the future. The models I chose to use were linear regression models to predict what the weather would look like globally for every season and every year until the year 2300. The whole idea was to get an insight as to what our normal temperatures for our seasons would look like in the future. I accomplished this through the use of training the linear regression model on the current data and using it to predict for the years it had and the years in the future. I validated it by putting the linear regression on the scatter plot of the actual temperature for that year and the models all did well but could have better predicted the drastic increses on the later years. I also investigated which countries were doing the best and worst in regards to global warming. The goal of this notebook is to help the reader come to the same conclusion I did which is that we need to do something about global warming and climate change sooner rather than later

    (9,10,11): "F"
