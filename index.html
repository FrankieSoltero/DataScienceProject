Project Goals


The goal of this project is to investigate whether climate change is as bad as it seems. My data set consists of all the average land temperatures around the globe, by every major city, by every country, by every city, and by every state. The goal will be to identify if there is tremendous increase in temperature from 1750 to today and to identify what caused those temperature increases. The data is sourced from https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/berkeleyearth/climate-change-earth-surface-temperature-data?resource=download. My hypothesis is that every country, city, major city, state, and the world as a whole will have a fairly large temperature increase from the first recorded temperatures. With a goal in mind to try and predict the global increase and country increase for the future. Ideally a model that would be able to predict just how how it will be in 50 years from now.


Website: https://frankiesoltero.github.io/DataScienceProject/






















Data Frames: Below in our code cell we see our 5 defined data frames.


global_temperatures: Contains the global temperatures from 1750 - 2015, we will use this for a future model to predict the temperature for years after 2015. Ideally would want to use a KNN model to predict the temp of the first of the month and from there compare it to the actual temperature of that day for the globe to validate the model


global_land_temps_bycity: This contains the land temperatures by city and the average temperature uncertainty for every city in the world. This data set will be useful to try and compare cities that are suffering from climate change. I would likely like to target a model that could predict what city you are in based on the date you are there and the temperature of that day.


global_land_temps_bycountry: This is the same idea as the city data frame, except it contains every country in the world. This will be very useful for identifying which country has had the overall largest increase in average temperature over time. Would be able to rank the best versus the worst countries in terms of biggest difference in temperature gain or loss.


global_land_temps_by_major_city: The same idea as per city except uses all the major cities in the world. This will be ideal for identifying major cities with high temperature increases and or decreases.


global_land_temps_by_state: Ideal for comparing the situations in regard to average temperature in the states. Will be useful for identifying what states are in the worst boat and if it has any correlation with their beliefs in climate change.

global_land_temps_bycountry['dt'] = pd.to_datetime(global_land_temps_bycountry['dt'])
temps_df = global_land_temps_bycountry
# Here we group by country and year and then use the average temperature and take the mean to get the year totals, and then we rename our indexes for clarity
temps_df = temps_df.groupby([temps_df['Country'], temps_df['dt'].dt.year]).agg({'AverageTemperature':'mean'}).rename_axis(index=['Country', 'Year']).reset_index()
country = input("What Country Would You Like to Investigate? ")
temps_df[temps_df['Country'] == country].plot.line(title=country, y='AverageTemperature', x='Year', xlabel='Year', ylabel='Average Temperature in Celsius')
